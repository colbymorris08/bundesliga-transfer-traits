# Independent Study Proposal 

**Working title:** Which traits travel into the Bundesliga — and which source leagues should scouts prioritize?  
**Scope:** Bundesliga destination only (narrow one-league inbound study)

---

## Research question

When players transfer **into the Bundesliga** from other leagues, which performance traits remain **stable vs. league-context**, and which **source leagues** produce the most effective arrivals?

Two evidence layers:

| Tier | Role | Data backbone | Headline N |
|------|------|---------------|------------|
| **Tier 1 — Wide (FBref)** | Prior → first Bundesliga Y1 at scale | **FBref** player-season stats across **19 leagues** | **329** analysis-ready pairs |
| **Tier 2 — Deep (StatsBomb)** | Event-level portability check on the same inbound logic | **StatsBomb Open Data** (17 competitions as priors) | **96** cohort players |

This extends the NYRB scouting-metric framework (**stability → redundancy → pizza / profiles → success**), focused on **which stats travel well** from the prior league into the Bundesliga — not Transfermarkt market value.

Inbound players are identified from **FBref season records** (first Bundesliga season / prior league ≠ Bundesliga), not from scraped Transfermarkt transfer tables.

---

**FBref** (Sports Reference) is the primary public dataset for scale metrics: viewable online; usable for coursework with attribution and rate-limited access. Respect their terms; do not redistribute raw dumps beyond the course.

---

## Design (Bundesliga only)

### FBref — scale layer (N = 329)

- **Who:** Players with a first **1. Bundesliga** season in end-years **2021–2025**, with prior and Y1 minutes ≥ **300′**, and a usable prior season outside 1. BL.
- **Where priors come from:** season tables across **19 leagues** (Big Five + 14 feeders), plus player-page fills where a league dump did not already cover the inbound.
- **What we test:** per-90 / rate metrics → **105** tested → category stability gates → **43** gated → light redundancy → **32** shortlisted.
- **Why:** Honest multi-league N for “which traits travel?” and “which feeders look strongest on Y1 minutes?”

### StatsBomb — event deep dive (N = 96)

- **Who:** Same inbound logic on open data; BL open dumps only **2015/16 + 2023/24**; priors from **all male** open competitions/seasons available (**17** competitions in the working set). Minute floors stay on (relaxed for open-data thinness): prior ≥ **45′**, BL Y1 ≥ **30′**.
- **Pipeline (same order):**
  1. **Stability** — correlate prior vs Bundesliga Y1 per-90. Gate **r ≥ 0.40** → **7 of 16** metrics pass.
  2. **Redundancy** — near-duplicates only at **|r| ≥ 0.85** (so e.g. passes stays with progressive passes) → **7 kept**.
  3. **Pizza / explorer** — profiles from that shortlist; success tests use **Y1 minutes**.
- **Role:** Event-level confirmation of which trait *families* travel — not a second league-ranking study. Open-data priors are mixed (club + international), so StatsBomb is read as noisier than FBref.

**Outputs:** traits that transferred well (stability), a short non-redundant scout card (redundancy), interactive explorer / pizza charts, and feeder-league / trait→Y1 success readouts.

---

## Methodology (brief)

The core idea is test–retest reliability across a **league change**, not across seasons in the same league. For each per-90 metric we compute Pearson *r* between a player’s prior-league season and their first Bundesliga season.

**FBref gates (category-specific):** Attacking / Passing / Other **r ≥ 0.60**; Defending / Carrying **r ≥ 0.50**. That yields **43** metrics from **105** tested. Redundancy at **|r| ≥ 0.95** (pick order: stability → sample size → prefer rate/contextual), with force-keeps (**SoT/90**, **Gls/90**, **Tkld Take**, **Att Total**) → final shortlist **32**.

**StatsBomb gate:** **r ≥ 0.40** → **7** metrics; redundancy **|r| ≥ 0.85** → keep all **7** (including passes).

**League-relative scaling** (within-league z / percentile) was tested on FBref rate→Y1 stability and **did not help**, so absolute per-90 rates were kept for both sources.

Inbound status is inferred from playing records (first Bundesliga season / prior competition ≠ 1. BL). Success proxy for Phase 2 = **Year-1 Bundesliga minutes** (playing-time success), not market value.

**Sample:** Destination = **1. Bundesliga** only. FBref first-BL window = end years **2021–2025**, with the **minutes restriction** still applied (prior ≥ **300′** and Y1 ≥ **300′**) — that is how we get analysis-ready **N = 329**, not every BL appearance. StatsBomb keeps its own lower floors (prior ≥ **45′**, Y1 ≥ **30′**) on open-data priors as above.

## Steps

- Build the inbound cohort: first 1. BL season in the study window, prior season ≠ 1. BL, **and** clear the minutes floors (FBref prior/Y1 ≥ **300′**; StatsBomb prior ≥ **45′** / Y1 ≥ **30′**); pull that prior-league season’s stats  

- **Stability** — correlate prior → BL Year-1; choose source-specific thresholds so we keep enough metrics without pretending weak correlations are portable  
- **Redundancy** — drop near-duplicates with contextual keep rules; shortlist the scout card (**32** FBref · **7** StatsBomb)  
- **Profiles & significance** — explorer / pizza graphics; Spearman trait & league tests vs **Y1 minutes**  

---

## Why this works?

- **Honest N:** FBref **329** paired seasons for the scale story; StatsBomb **96** for event depth — not a claim that open data alone has hundreds of clean feeder→BL pairs.  
- **Narrow:** only into the Bundesliga.  
- **Clear pipeline:** stability → thresholds → redundancy → explorer → significance.  
- **Multi-league FBref:** same method beyond Big-5-only anecdotes.  
- **Accurate where it matters:** FBref for portable rates + feeder readouts; StatsBomb as a smaller, deeper event check.
